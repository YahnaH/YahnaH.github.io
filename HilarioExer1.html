Have you ever had a discussion that was more of a kwentuhan session than a class?


Perhaps if I were to associate 1 word with my KAS 1 subject it would be "kwentuhan"

It was one of the GEs that I was able to have during the pre-pandemic and we had face-to-face sessions for this one.

It was like having coffee with your prof and he'd tell us stories not only about the past but some "teas" or some beefs between

one historian and the other. Before I took Computer Science as a course, I took BA History, and if there would be some fun takeaway

between people and history, is that historians have a lot of beef with each other and they feud in the classiest and academic way

I know, through writing papers and rebutting each other. Take Teodoro Agonicillo whose works we had to read and whose "heated arguments"

with some of the Filipino Historians were documented through their writings.


However, drama aside, through KAS 1 was I really able to appreciate our pre-colonial roots and gain better respect to our ancestors

Perhaps it was through KAS 1 that I was able to see how the Spaniards abused us and how they used Christianity as a means to oppress

us and deny us of our rights to our own land. I was also able to gain a better vantage of how women were placed in society during the

pre-colonial period and how we were set aside and placed in lower regard during the Spanish colonization.

To sum it all up, this course became the entry-point to my ongoing curiousity and awakening to our roots.

History provides a window for us to not only see, but also learn from our truths.
